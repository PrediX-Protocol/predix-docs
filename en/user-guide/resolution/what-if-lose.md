# What If I Lose?

## Short Answer

Your losing shares become worth $0. You lose what you paid for them — nothing more.

## Example

```
You bought: 100 YES shares at $0.35 → Cost: $35
Market resolves: NO wins
Your YES shares: $0.00
Loss: $35

You will NOT lose more than $35.
There is NO negative balance.
There are NO margin calls.
```

## Can I Reduce My Loss?

**Yes — sell before the market ends.**

```
Bought YES at $0.35
Price drops to $0.15 (things looking bad for YES)

Option A: Hold → if NO wins, lose $0.35/share
Option B: Sell at $0.15 → lose only $0.20/share (saved $0.15)
```

> 💡 **Tip**: If you change your mind about a prediction, sell early to limit losses.

## Maximum Loss

Your maximum loss is **always** the amount you paid:

| Bought at | Max loss per share | Max gain per share |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| $0.10 | $0.10 | $0.90 |
| $0.50 | $0.50 | $0.50 |
| $0.90 | $0.90 | $0.10 |

## Do I Need to Do Anything?

**No.** Losing shares simply have no value. They stay in your wallet but are worth $0. You don't need to "close" or "settle" anything.
