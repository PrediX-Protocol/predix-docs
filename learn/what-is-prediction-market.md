# What is a Prediction Market?

A prediction market lets you **bet on what will happen in the real world** — and get paid if you're right.

## Simple Example

> **Market**: "Will Bitcoin be above $100,000 by December 31?"

- You think **YES** → Buy YES shares at $0.40 each
- Market ends, BTC is $105,000 → **YES wins!**
- Your YES shares are now worth **$1.00 each**
- You paid $0.40, got back $1.00 → **Profit: $0.60 per share**

If BTC was only $95,000? Your YES shares = $0. You lose your $0.40.

## How is This Different from Betting?

| | Traditional Betting | Prediction Markets |
|---|---|---|
| Who sets the odds? | The house (bookmaker) | The market (other traders) |
| Can you sell early? | ❌ Usually not | ✅ Yes, anytime |
| Who are you betting against? | The house | Other traders |
| Transparency | Hidden odds | Prices visible on-chain |
| Your funds | Held by company | In your own wallet |

## Key Idea: Price = Probability

A share price tells you what the market thinks the chance is:

| Price | Market thinks... |
|-------|-----------------|
| $0.10 | 10% chance of happening |
| $0.50 | 50% — coin flip |
| $0.80 | 80% — very likely |
| $0.95 | 95% — almost certain |

**If you disagree with the market, you can profit.** Market says 30% but you think it's 70%? Buy at $0.30, potentially win $1.00.

## What Can You Trade?

- 🏦 **Crypto**: "Will ETH hit $10K?"
- 🗳️ **Politics**: "Who wins the next election?"
- ⚽ **Sports**: "Chelsea vs Man City — who wins?"
- 🌍 **World Events**: "Will there be a ceasefire by June?"
- 📊 **Economics**: "Will the Fed cut rates?"

## The Two Outcomes: YES and NO

Every market has two tokens:

- **YES token**: worth $1 if the event happens, $0 if not
- **NO token**: worth $1 if the event doesn't happen, $0 if it does

Together, YES + NO always ≈ $1 (because one of them MUST win).
