# How Prices Work

## Price = What Traders Are Willing to Pay

There's no central authority setting prices. **Traders set the price** by buying and selling.

If many people buy YES → YES price goes up (more people think it will happen).
If many people sell YES → YES price goes down (people losing confidence).

## Reading the Price

```
Market: "Will BTC hit $100K by year end?"

YES price: $0.38 → Market thinks 38% chance
NO price:  $0.62 → Market thinks 62% it won't happen
```

> 💡 **Your edge**: If you believe the real chance is higher or lower than the market price, you can profit by trading.

## How You Make Money

### Scenario 1: You're Right

```
You buy 100 YES shares at $0.38 each → Cost: $38
Market resolves YES → Each share worth $1.00
You receive: $100
Profit: $100 - $38 = $62 ✅
```

### Scenario 2: You're Wrong

```
You buy 100 YES shares at $0.38 each → Cost: $38
Market resolves NO → Each share worth $0.00
You receive: $0
Loss: -$38 ❌
```

### Scenario 3: Sell Early

```
You buy 100 YES shares at $0.38
Price moves to $0.55 (good news for YES)
You sell at $0.55 → Receive: $55
Profit: $55 - $38 = $17 ✅
(Without waiting for market to end!)
```

## The $1 Rule

> ⚠️ **Important**: YES price + NO price ≈ $1.00 (always)

Why? Because 1 YES + 1 NO = $1 guaranteed. If YES + NO < $1, traders buy both and profit from the difference. This keeps prices aligned.

## Price Moves

| Event | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| Good news for outcome | YES price ↑, NO price ↓ |
| Bad news for outcome | YES price ↓, NO price ↑ |
| Uncertainty increases | Both move toward $0.50 |
| Outcome almost certain | YES → $0.95+, NO → $0.05 |
